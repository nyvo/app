Full-bleed cinematic

Image fills the viewport at 60vh. Title overlaid bottom-left on a dark scrim.

  • Pros: dramatic, marketing-feel
  • Cons: image stretches edge-to-edge — quality degrades on ultrawide; takes 60% of the viewport regardless of screen size; title set in white so it depends on hero darkness

Layout A · proposed

Editorial — contained image, title below

Image confined to the page container with a fixed 21:9 aspect ratio. Title and meta drop below the image like a magazine feature.

  • Image never stretches past 1280px — quality stays consistent on ultrawide
  • Title is dark text on light bg, so it doesn't depend on the image being dark
  • Hero takes less vertical space — the booking panel arrives sooner on mobile
  • Loses some of the "cinematic" punch of full-bleed

Layout B · proposed

Asymmetric split — image left, content right

Portrait-aspect image on the left, title and meta on the right. Image keeps a fixed aspect inside its column, content sits at a comfortable reading width even on huge displays. Stacks vertically on mobile.

  • Most scalable of the three — no full-width imagery anywhere, content has a hard reading-width cap
  • Image stays high-fidelity at any viewport because its container width is bounded
  • Content and image share equal weight — feels more like a product page than a marketing hero
  • Less dramatic than full-bleed
