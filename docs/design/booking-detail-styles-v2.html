The booking detail page, three ways

Same course, same data, three complete pages. Everything the structure doc locked is held constant: tokens verbatim (Geist, hue-250 neutrals, hairlines, black pill CTA, azure only as selection), real fees from pricing.ts (2 200 kr → 110 kr, 250 kr → 13 kr), checkout's radio-dot grammar, seller-branded header (Q2), the policy field (Q4), and the edge-state rules (they render identically in all three, so they're not repeated here). What varies is style: where the booking lives, how loud the identity is, and how far the expression dial moves. All three booking zones are clickable. Artwork is live placeholder photography.

S1Rolig — calm single column, booking embedded in the flow

The quiet default. Eventbrite's event-page order (date over title, organizer bar, «when and where», policy at the end) rendered in our system, with the booking zone as Luma's flat embedded fill instead of a floating card. Nothing on the page is elevated; the grey fill alone marks the decision.

Reference extraction — S1

Structural question: how does a calm, trust-first event page order identity, facts, booking and reassurance in one column?

Sources: Eventbrite event page (date eyebrow over title, organizer bar, «When and where» two-up, refund policy as a section), Luma event page (registration as a soft embedded fill in the reading flow).

Adopted: (1) date line over the title, quiet; (2) organizer bar as a panel-fill strip with the instructor and one link; (3) «Når og hvor» as a two-column text block, no icon sprinkle; (4) booking = flat fill, zero elevation; (5) «Greit å vite» closes the page (Q4's policy field, Eventbrite's refund-policy slot).

Rejected: Eventbrite's floating right card (S3's job); follower counts and Follow buttons (no such data).

Reference extraction — S3

Structural question: when the transaction lives in a persistent rail, what does the rail carry and what stays in the reading column?

Sources: Care.com activity page (photo + title left; rail right with a «Series | Drop-ins» segmented toggle and per-session prices), Airbnb availability rail (sticky, self-sufficient, one action), app BookingRailLite.tsx (the shipped rail: «Når» context field, tier tiles, fee summary, black CTA).

Adopted: (1) the rail is self-sufficient — when, offer, price, action; (2) the tier choice is the SegmentedTabs toggle (Care.com's own pattern at this exact cardinality); (3) scarcity sits in the rail above the CTA; (4) the column keeps only story content: title, host, about, schedule.

Rejected: Care.com's per-session select buttons (drop-in auto-picks the next session today); review stars and tabs (no such data).
