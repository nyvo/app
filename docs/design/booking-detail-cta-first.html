Kursside — CTA-first (v3.1) — T2 retired; T1 refined; Stripe Payment Element + receipt summary

T1 vs T3: «one CTA, then billett + skjema + betaling»

Model confirmed by the research pass: event products (Eventbrite, DICE, Zomato, Open) put no tier selection on the detail page — it shows the prices as content and one action; step two is a single focused screen holding ticket choice, guest details and payment together. Both takes share that skeleton and the system (tokens verbatim, real fees, floating-label inputs). New in v3.1: payment renders as the real Stripe Payment Element in tabs layout — method cards where selecting one re-renders the pane below (card → fields; a wallet swaps the bottom pay button for its own branded one, so the action never moves) — and the order summary reads as a receipt: price line, fee line, total. Wallet buttons are the payment brands' own assets and sit outside our tokens by design. Step two is clickable everywhere; detail CTAs scroll to their step two.

T1Magasin — image-led feature page, booking strip, focused checkout

The detail page reads like a magazine feature: wide hero, display title, and a scannable metadata row directly under it — when, where and instructor as two-line stacks with a leading badge or icon (Luma's fact tiles in V3's hairline fact-band structure — framed, divided, no cards), collapsing to a stacked list on narrow viewports. Commerce is the page's only elevated surface — flat hairlines everywhere, and exactly one floating card for the object that takes money (the system's focal-surface rule). Inside it the page's own grammar continues: information column flush-left (label, price, drop-in, scarcity), single action right. Airbnb/Luma supplied the structure; the skin is derived from this page. Location gets its own «Sted» section further down, because the map is functional — Google Places is already wired in the app. The schedule is a flickable strip of date cards. Step two is Open's calm, single-column «complete your booking» screen: the prebuilt segmented toggle for the two tiers, the Stripe Payment Element in tabs layout, and a receipt-style summary.

Reference extraction — T1

Structural question: what does a detail page owe the buyer when selection moves to step two, and what does the focused step-two screen contain?

Sources: Eventbrite purchase flow (detail = price range + «Get tickets»; step 2 = tiers, then form, then pay), Open booking flow (editorial class page → one «Book» → focused checkout with details + payment), MasterClass (price-first offer block).

Adopted: (1) both prices printed in the strip — nobody clicks blind; (2) date cards replace the numbered session list (Posh's date-card strip); (3) step two = one screen: billett → opplysninger → betaling, no wizard; (4) cancellation reassurance sits under the pay button, where Open puts its policy; (5) payment = the Stripe Payment Element, tabs layout — method cards where the selected method's pane renders below; a wallet tab renders the wallet's own button instead of fields (Stripe's default behavior); (6) the summary is a receipt: one line per amount, total last; (7) started courses change values, never layout: the strip only updates label + price («Kurspakke 1 650 kr»), held sessions are marked on the date cards, and the receipt alone carries the story — ordinær pris, named deduction (green, Typeform's coupon-line grammar), result.

Rejected: quantity steppers (one seat per booking); a persistent mini-bar on the detail page (the strip is one scroll away, and mobile keeps the CTA visible early); wallets-above-the-form with an «eller» divider (Eventbrite's variant — valid, but the tabs element keeps one payment zone).

Reference extraction — T3

Structural question: what does the split gain when the same two-column grammar carries the buyer from the detail page through payment?

Sources: Stripe Checkout (product — dark summary column left, form right; the canonical split checkout), StubHub purchase flow (persistent order context above the payment step), Open (context header carried into checkout).

Adopted: (1) one grammar for both steps — the band is the constant, the right column changes job; (2) the band's step-two summary re-renders live on ticket switch (Stripe's summary column); (3) cancellation note lives in the band, always visible; (4) the tier choice is the prebuilt segmented toggle — the same control as the dashboard filters.

Rejected: Stripe's dark neutral (the band borrows the studio's color instead — the seller is the brand here); a third column; any selection on step one.
